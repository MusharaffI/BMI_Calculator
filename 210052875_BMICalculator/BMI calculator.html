BMI Guide

  • Your BMI is less than 18.5 = Underweight
  • Your BMI is between 18.5 and 24.9 BMI = Healthy
  • Your BMI is between 25 To 29.9 BMI = Overweight
  • Your BMI is 30 Or Greater = Obese
Please ensure you understand that BMI is not always an accurate measurement for your health.
The purpose of BMI is to be an indicator of body fatness.
It is often reliable, however there are times when it is not accurate.
This calculation may overestimate your BMI in these circumstances:
  • Body builders
  • Athletes
  • Pregnant women
This calculation may also underestimate your BMI in these circumstances:
  • The elderly
  • People with physical disabilities
BMI is also not an accurate indicator for people with:
  • Extreme Obesity
  • Eating disorders like anorexia
The calculation for BMI is done by inputting you weight in kilograms then dividing by your height in metres squared.
